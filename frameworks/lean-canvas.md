# Lean Canvas Framework

## Overview
The Lean Canvas is a one-page business model adapted by Ash Maurya from the Business Model Canvas. It's designed specifically for startups and focuses on addressing uncertainty and risk.

## When to Use
- **Early stage**: Validating a new business idea
- **Startup**: Defining a new product or company
- **Pre-build**: Before writing code or building product
- **Pivot**: Reassessing business model after learning

## The 9 Building Blocks

### 1. Problem
**Top 3 problems your customers have**
- List the top 1-3 problems
- Include existing alternatives (how they solve it today)
- Identify the current workarounds

### 2. Customer Segments
**Target customers and users**
- Identify specific customer segments
- Define early adopters (who will buy first)
- Be specific: demographics, firmographics, behavior

### 3. Unique Value Proposition
**Single, clear, compelling message**
- Why you are different and worth buying
- What's the high-level concept
- Should be clear from this statement alone what you do

### 4. Solution
**Top 3 features for your MVP**
- Minimum feature set required to solve the problem
- Not all features, just the essential ones
- Directly maps to problems listed

### 5. Channels
**Path to customers**
- How will you reach your customers
- Free vs. paid channels
- Inbound vs. outbound
- Direct vs. indirect

### 6. Revenue Streams
**How you will make money**
- Revenue model (subscription, license, transaction, etc.)
- Pricing strategy
- Lifetime value (LTV)

### 7. Cost Structure
**Customer acquisition costs and other costs**
- Fixed costs
- Variable costs
- Cost of customer acquisition (CAC)
- Key cost drivers

### 8. Key Metrics
**Key activities you measure**
- One or two key numbers that tell you how your business is doing
- Leading indicators of success
- What metric moves the needle

### 9. Unfair Advantage
**Something that cannot be easily copied or bought**
- Real competitive advantage (not just "better service")
- Network effects, proprietary technology, insider information
- "Can't be easily copied" is the test
- It's OK if you don't have one yet (most don't)

## Validation Sequence

### Order of Validation (Riskiest First)
1. **Problem-Solution Fit**: Do customers have this problem? Will they pay for this solution?
2. **Product-Market Fit**: Can you build something customers want at scale?
3. **Scale**: Can you grow profitably?

### Testing Approach
- **Problem**: Customer interviews (10-15 minimum)
- **Solution**: Mockups, prototypes, smoke tests
- **Price**: Pre-orders, landing page with price
- **Channels**: Small paid experiments
- **Economics**: Calculate unit economics early

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Too many features in solution**
→ Focus on top 3 that solve top 3 problems

❌ **"Everyone" as customer segment**
→ Be specific, identify early adopters

❌ **Vague value proposition**
→ Clear, single sentence

❌ **"Better/Faster/Cheaper" as unfair advantage**
→ These can be copied; find something unique

❌ **Skipping cost structure**
→ Unit economics matter from day one

❌ **Confusing assumptions with facts**
→ Mark everything as assumption until validated

## Integration with Agent

The validation agent uses Lean Canvas as the output format because:
1. **One page**: Forces clarity and brevity
2. **Assumption-focused**: Highlights what needs testing
3. **Action-oriented**: Directly maps to validation experiments
4. **Widely understood**: Common language for product teams
5. **Fast to create**: Can be completed in one session
